I'm a PhD student at UM6P modelling cultural transmission by focusing on teacher decision-making. Using an agent-based framework, I investigate how cost-benefit tradeoffs influence who gets taught, and how that shapes both cultural and genetic continuity. My research has implications for education, public policy, and even social equity. My supervisors are Ed Seabright and Sarah Alami at the School of Collective Intelligence.

I am also a collective intelligence specialist who facilitates group sessions on solving wicked problems and designs collective intelligence systems and processes.
